About

I help nonprofits build the revenue infrastructure they deserve.

I'm a seasoned nonprofit executive, fundraiser, and strategist with more than 25 years of impact-driven leadership across advocacy, community organizing, and philanthropy. I've built organizations from the ground up, reversed major financial deficits, and driven multi-million-dollar revenue growth for causes that matter.

My approach is relational and donor-centered — I believe every organization's greatest untapped asset is the relationships it already has. I help you map, cultivate, and unlock those relationships through systems that outlast any single campaign.

As a former National Vice President for Development at Common Cause, I oversaw national development operations, rebuilt direct mail programs, and closed six-figure gifts. As Principal of López-Calderón Consulting, I've partnered with organizations from $300K to multi-million-dollar budgets — meeting them exactly where they are.

I hold a J.D. from DePaul University and a Philosophy degree from the University of Chicago. I'm a trainer, facilitator, and coalition-builder who brings both rigor and heart to every engagement — and I work across the political spectrum, partnering with organizations wherever they stand on the issues.

Turning Deficits into Surpluses

At Common Cause, I inherited a development operation running a $2 million deficit. I rebuilt the national fundraising infrastructure from the ground up — overhauling the direct mail program, re-engaging lapsed donors, and cultivating a portfolio of 150+ mid-level through six-figure supporters across a membership base of more than 100,000.
